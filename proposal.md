# Mini Project 2 Proposal
**What I'm building:**  
I am building a text analysis app that compares how different news platforms describe conflicts, protests, and political events through word choice.

**Why I chose this:**  
This project comes from the political thought idea of “who has the right to narrate,” connected to Frantz Fanon and Edward Said’s “Permission to Narrate.” I want to study whether media platforms repeatedly use negatively connotated words to describe certain groups or events, shaping how readers understand them.

**Core features:**
- Allow users to paste article text into the app.
- Count and identify emotionally charged or negatively connotated words.
- Compare word choices across different news sources.
- Show basic statistics such as word frequency, negative-word ratio, and most common framing words.
- Visualize the results using charts.

**What I don't know yet:**
- Which news APIs will allow easy access to article text.
- Whether I should start with pasted text or API-pulled articles.
- How to define the list of “negative framing” words fairly.
- How to compare articles without oversimplifying complex political language.
